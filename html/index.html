We understand that we are tackling a complex systemic issue, so this informs our approach to this work

(right now we are starting...) Because of COVID-19, millions of students are falling behind, and stand to lose up to a year's worth of education. These students, being predominantly of low income and systemically disadvantaged, are stuck at home without computers, internet, or a dedicated place where they can do their work. Without intervention, these students are expected to lose a year of schooling. they need this now! invest in our future invest in our children! move us toward our dream. set up the mill. build programs and study spaces for them... probaly mention in mission Pythagoras Ecology Works is a California non-profit established specifically to provide a net greenhouse gas reduction through the operation of a zero emissions sawmill works, ecology/STEAM education projects for k-12/college students in target area and useful wood products that benefit the region and community. Every piece of this project is intentional. ​​Each of our activities are directly tied to carbon sequestration, improved education, health and community resources. In 2018, governer Jerry Brown declared a state of emergency in the tree mortality crisis with more than 120 million trees killed by record drought & insects. In order to mitigate further risk of wildfires, the state planned to remove downed dead trees near roadways, utility lines that pose a threat to public safety first. We will be partnering with local public works departments, landowners, businesses, and citizens to remove and mill downed trees before the wood looses its’ quality as lumber. Logs which cannot be milled will be repurposed for particleboard bio-mass wood chip gasification to operate the sawmill plants while reducing our waste and carbon footprint. We will produce wood products that are of service in construction and education (desks, chairs, worktables) which are elegant, portable, and functional. The zero emissions sawmill is designed to produce 2mbf per eight-hour shift. The entire operation is set to use renewable energy sources to power its equipment. The site location will be in Stockton, California and serve the areas between Fresno and Stockton. The project is replicable and scalable. The location we have selected to launch our initiative on is downtown Fresno. As our program expands and grows, we also hope to extend our reach to provide learning spaces for youth in disadvantaged or disaster-affected areas across the United States. The nationwide need for CLT lumber is growing, with capacity built into project design, we will be well positioned to participate as supplier to this market: first by providing mass wood CLT, particle board, plywood, plywood veneer products; harvesting most material from defined emergency zone areas, helping create healthy forest as outlined in CAL Fire priority activity guidelines. Through research, the teaching and learning communities developed will create new products based on testing and applicable uses for fencing, gardening containers, urban raised bed vegetable gardens, covered community spaces, outdoor seating, tables. In addition to our student- focused programming, we are also pursuing $75/ $25 USDA rural utilities infrastructure match grants so we can utilize our nail-free, zero emission, recycled wood designs to develop 5,000+sq.ft. homes for youth who are aging out of foster care. On our timeline, limited production of structures would begin to be delivered in the second year of operations. You can't work from home if you don't have a desk or a computer Millions of students are stuck at home without computers, internet, or a dedicated place where they can do their work. Most of these students are from low-income, and/or systematically disadvantaged groups. Without intervention, these students are expected to loose a year of schooling. This will place a burden on educators, as they try to adapt their curriculum to a populace of students who were, but are no longer, in the same learning cohort. Further, and perhaps more importantly, being so far behind can be so disheartening, that these students may not feel as though they can catch up, and may become disengaged with education long-term. Millions of Students are falling behind, and stand to loose up to a year's worth of education. We are tackling a systematic issue, so we are built a complex adaptive system. We proposed to solve two problems at once: getting rid of the dead trees that cause wildfires, and building dignified workstations for the community. We requested permission to mill a batch of dead trees from central California (the same ones that cause wildfires). Permission was granted. We reached out to contacts than have agreed to let us host the logs, and process it into plywood. By using pythagorean geometry, we can laser cut the wood into desks and chairs that can be flat packed and assembled on site. We are working with a consortium of churches and community organizations (such as the YMCA) to put these workstations in places where students can access them. To mitigate the fact that many students who have been told to work from home to not have a computer, we propose using end computing to enable one computer to serve 6 students. Virtual machines would magnify the ability of the one computer, and amplify the affect that we would be able to have with the resources at our disposal, or within the community.
